# 5W1H Framework for Research Ideation

## Overview

The 5W1H framework is a systematic thinking tool that helps researchers construct and evaluate research ideas from multiple dimensions. By answering six key questions, you can rapidly build a comprehensive understanding of a research topic.

## Framework Components

### What

**Core question**: What problem or phenomenon do you want to study?

**Thinking dimensions**:
- What is the specific research subject?
- What is the core problem to be solved?
- What are the scope and boundaries of the research?
- What are the expected research outcomes?

**Examples** (see `references/domain.md` "Example 5W1H" for domain-appropriate examples):
- Clearly names the research subject, problem, scope, and expected outcomes

### Why

**Core question**: Why is this problem important?

**Thinking dimensions**:
- What is the academic value of this problem?
- What practical significance does solving this problem have?
- What are the limitations of current research?
- Why is now a good time to study this problem?

**Evaluation criteria**:
- **Academic importance**: Does it advance the field?
- **Practical impact**: Does it solve a real-world problem?
- **Timeliness**: Does it align with current research trends?
- **Feasibility**: Are there sufficient resources and technical support?

### Who

**Core question**: Who are the target audience and stakeholders?

**Thinking dimensions**:
- Who would care about this research?
- Who would use the research outcomes?
- Who would benefit from it?
- Who do you need to collaborate with?

**Stakeholder categories**:
- **Academia**: Same-field researchers, reviewers, citers
- **Industry**: Product teams, engineers, decision-makers
- **Users**: End users, affected groups
- **Collaborators**: Advisors, colleagues, interdisciplinary experts

### When

**Core question**: What is the time horizon and context for the research?

**Thinking dimensions**:
- What is the expected duration of the research?
- When is the best time to pursue this research?
- When will the relevant technology and data be available?
- When are outcomes expected?

**Time planning**:
- **Short-term** (3-6 months): Initial exploration, literature review, method validation
- **Mid-term** (6-12 months): Full experiments, paper writing, submission
- **Long-term** (1-2 years): Systematic research, multiple papers, theoretical contributions

### Where

**Core question**: What are the application scenarios and domains?

**Thinking dimensions**:
- In which specific scenarios does the research apply?
- In which domains or industries does it have value?
- Does geographic or cultural context matter?
- Where will experiments be conducted?

**Application scenario categories**:
- **Academic scenarios**: Theory validation, method comparison, benchmarking
- **Industrial scenarios**: Product development, system optimization, problem diagnosis
- **Societal scenarios**: Policy making, public services, social impact
- **Cross-domain**: Multidisciplinary intersections, technology transfer

### How

**Core question**: What is the preliminary approach or methodology?

**Thinking dimensions**:
- What research method or technical approach will be used?
- What data and resources are needed?
- How will the research hypothesis be validated?
- What are the expected technical challenges?

**Method types**:
- **Theoretical research**: Mathematical proofs, theoretical analysis, model derivation
- **Experimental research**: Algorithm design, experimental validation, performance evaluation
- **Applied research**: System development, case studies, user testing
- **Survey research**: Literature reviews, meta-analysis, systematic evaluation

## Best Practices

### 1. Iterative Thinking

Do not expect to answer all questions in a single pass. Quickly go through all 6 questions first, then iterate and refine repeatedly.

### 2. Priority Ordering

Adjust focus based on the research stage:
- **Early stage**: Focus on What, Why, Who
- **Mid stage**: Focus on How, When, Where
- **Late stage**: Comprehensive review of all dimensions

### 3. Documentation

Record the 5W1H analysis results as the foundation for the research proposal.
